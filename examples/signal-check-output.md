## Signal Check: "Deep Work in a Distraction Economy"

> **Target:** Blog post draft | **Mode:** Full analysis | **Axes:** 4

### Axis 1: Meaning
| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Core claim** | Remote work inherited broken office habits; protecting deep work hours is the fix |
| **Originality** | Medium — Cal Newport framework is known, but the remote-work application and "4-hour rule" framing add value |
| **Depth** | Good for a blog post. Three actionable shifts, each with a concrete implementation step |
| **Gap:** | The "distraction economy" framing in the title isn't fully developed in the body — only touched in intro and outro |

### Axis 2: Facts
| Claim | Status |
|-------|--------|
| "Average knowledge worker checks email every 6 minutes" | Asserted — needs source. Original research (Gonzalez & Mark, UC Irvine) found ~every 6 min for task-switching, not specifically email |
| "4 hours of concentrated effort per day" | Plausible — aligns with Ericsson's deliberate practice research, but the claim is simplified |
| Cal Newport's deep work definition | Accurate quote |

**Recommendation:** Add a source for the email stat or soften to "studies suggest." The 4-hour claim should reference its origin.

### Axis 3: Framing
| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Rhetoric** | Strong opening hook. Good use of contrast ("freedom" vs. "calendar full of calls") |
| **Assumptions** | Assumes reader is a knowledge worker with autonomy over their schedule. May not apply to customer-facing or shift-based remote roles |
| **Tone** | Direct, slightly provocative — fits the blog format well |
| **Structure** | Clean: problem → framework → 3 actions → constraint → CTA. No structural issues |

### Axis 4: Context
| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Timeliness** | Evergreen topic, but "post-pandemic remote" angle is becoming dated. Consider reframing as "remote-first" rather than "remote work" |
| **Scope** | Individual contributor focus. Missing: how managers can protect team deep work time |
| **Audience** | Well-targeted for individual knowledge workers. May miss managers who could amplify impact |

### Verdict

**Signal: Strong.** The post has a clear thesis, actionable structure, and good rhetoric. Three improvements would elevate it:

1. **Source the email stat** or soften the claim
2. **Develop the "distraction economy" thread** — it's promised in the title but underdelivered
3. **Add one sentence acknowledging** roles where full schedule autonomy isn't possible

### Robustness: 7/10
Solid draft. One revision pass addressing the above would bring it to publishing quality.
